Cropped sensors

In a camera there are different size sensors that recorded the image. There are many different sized sensors but when talking about a normal DSLR sensor there are three different sizes Medium format, Full frame, and APS-C also known as cropped. The largest sensor is medium format because it is the same size as medium format fill. The second sensor is full frame because its 35mm wide just like a traditional 35mm film camera. The smallest of those is APS-C (cropped sensor). It’s around because it’s cheaper to make a smaller sensor. Because the APS-C sensor is smaller it crops the images so when you use a same sized lens on a full frame camera and as a cropped sensor it will look bigger on the cropped sensor. For example a 50mm lens will become a 80mm lens on a cropped sensor.
